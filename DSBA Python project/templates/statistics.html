Hypothesis: whether there is a significant difference in the average points scored per game between different teams.

At the heart of any successful sports team is the ability to score points. But do certain NBA teams consistently outscore their opponents, or is the gap between teams more narrow than we think? In this analysis, we explore the hypothesis that there is a significant difference in average points scored per game between different NBA teams. Using data spanning from 1980 to 2022, we examine the average points scored by All-star players from each team during this timeframe. Our findings shed light on the consistency and parity of point scoring across different NBA teams over several decades.

As we can see, with data spanning from 1980 to 2022, the plot focuses on the average points scored by All-star players from each team during this timeframe. The results suggest that most teams are relatively equal in terms of average points scored, with some minor fluctuations for teams like the Charlotte Hornets, Detroit Pistons, New Jersey Nets, and Phoenix Suns due to rebuild processes that resulted in fewer All-star players during certain periods.
